On Growth

What lasts isn't the title — it's what you build and who you help.

I've moved across research, industry, and independent work — through more than one transition of my own. The part I care about most now is helping other people through theirs: the science, the career, the decision in front of them.

01

Helping people through transitions

Having navigated a competitive, multi-stage search myself, I spend time helping others through the same. The part that matters most to me: helping people see themselves through the eyes of the person hiring them — what to surface, how to frame both technical and soft skills, how to use AI tools with intention rather than as a shortcut.

In practice that looks like live mock interviews, technical deep-dives, and presentation run-throughs — the kind of real-time feedback no tool can replicate. In 2026 I've helped several people into pharma and biotech roles, coached from current experience.

02

Advisory & consulting

Advising As a scientific advisor on AI/ML at C10 Labs / AI BioHub — an MIT Media Lab spin-off — I advised early-stage biotech and pharma founders on scientific strategy, AI/ML maturity, and partnership readiness.

Consulting Independent strategy consulting for biotech — including helping a startup build a bioinformatics approach to using real-world data for target discovery.

04

Community

I organize and lead the Bioinformatics & AI XChange — a community where scientists present papers, trade what's real versus hype in AI, and stay current in a field that moves every week.

It grew over six years — starting with journal-club presentations, then evolving into a curated speaker series that bridges industry practitioners, academics, and cross-sector voices across career stages. A place where people learn out loud, together.
