The story

Origin, journey, fork, and where it all lands.

Origin

My father, a mechanical engineering professor, taught me to derive ideas from first principles — diagrams on napkins over dinner, never just the answer. I came to science through medicine first, working as an EMT. That experience made one thing clear: data only matters if it reaches people who can act on it, and how you explain something is as important as the finding itself.

Journey

I trained as a computational biologist and spent years turning multi-omics and clinical trial data into decisions in oncology. The science was always in service of something larger — understanding what drives disease, what changes outcomes, and what moves a finding from a dataset into a clinical program that reaches patients.

Fork

I stepped outside pharma to sit at the business development table, building more than twenty data and AI partnerships and seeing clinical development from the other side. It changed how I think about impact. Strategy, communication, and knowing what gets prioritized matters as much as what gets discovered. That's also when AI started reshaping what was possible.

Now

I came back with a dual lens — the technical depth of a data scientist and the strategic instinct of someone who's been in both rooms. At GSK I work at the intersection of clinical data, multi-omics, and AI: finding how AI can amplify and accelerate the science, and connecting functions that rarely speak the same language. The compliment I hold onto: "Celine, I feel smarter after coming out of your meetings and presentations."
